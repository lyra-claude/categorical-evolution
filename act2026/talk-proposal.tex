\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} Kleisli categories, genetic algorithms, lax monoidal functors, compositional invariants

\medskip

Hanks et al.\ showed that gradient descent is a hypergraph functor from optimization problems to dynamical systems~\cite{hanks2024}. Their framework requires differentiability. We provide the categorical foundation for the \emph{gradient-free} regime: population-based evolutionary search, where the relevant structure is not a derivative but a monad carrying randomness, configuration, and logging.

\textbf{Framework.}
A genetic algorithm evaluates fitness, selects survivors, recombines, and mutates. These operators are not pure functions --- they require random number generation, read-only parameters, and write-only logging. We package all three effects into a monad $M = \mathsf{ReaderT}\;\mathit{Config}\;(\mathsf{WriterT}\;\mathit{Log}\;(\mathsf{State}\;\mathit{StdGen}))$ and define a \emph{genetic operator} as a Kleisli arrow $\mathit{op} \colon [a] \to M\,[b]$ on populations. One generation step is the Kleisli composite
\[
  \mathit{step} = \mathit{evaluate} \ggg \mathit{select} \ggg \mathit{crossover} \ggg \mathit{mutate} \;\colon\; [a] \to M\,[a]
\]
and an $n$-generation run is $\mathit{step}^n$. The framework instantiates Miranda's abstract Kleisli structures~\cite{miranda2024} concretely: the Kleisli category $\mathcal{K}\!\ell(M)$ with population types as objects and GA operators as morphisms. We identify three composition levels --- operators into pipelines (one generation), pipelines across generations into strategies, strategies via higher-order combinators into multi-population systems --- each forming a category with the same Kleisli composition.

\textbf{Result 1: Diversity Fingerprints.}
Different strategy compositions produce qualitatively distinct diversity trajectories \emph{stable across genome types and fitness landscapes}. On three domains (real-valued weight vectors, binary mazes, expression trees), we identify four fingerprints: monotonic decline (generational), spike--crash--rebound (hourglass), stable maintenance (island/migration), and spike--collapse (adaptive/plateau-triggered). These are determined by the \emph{composition pattern}, not by the individual operators. We conjecture that fingerprints are functorial invariants of strategy composition, analogous to Myers' behavioral invariants of composed dynamical systems~\cite{myers2022}.

\textbf{Result 2: The Strict/Lax Dichotomy.}
The island model lifts a single-population strategy to a multi-population strategy with periodic migration, defining a functor $I \colon \mathbf{Strat} \to \mathbf{Strat}$.

\begin{theorem}[Strict/Lax Dichotomy]
For strategies $S_1, S_2$ and island functor $I_{\mu}$ with migration rate $\mu$:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item If $\mu = 0$: $I(S_1 \ggg S_2) = I(S_1) \ggg I(S_2)$ \emph{(strict monoidal)}.
  \item If $\mu > 0$: the laxator $\lambda \colon I(S_1) \ggg I(S_2) \Rightarrow I(S_1 \ggg S_2)$ has magnitude $D^*$ that is independent of composition boundary position and migration frequency \emph{(uniformly lax)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\noindent
The uniform laxity arises because resetting the migration schedule at the composition boundary perturbs a chaotic dynamical system; the perturbation magnitude is determined asymptotically by the spectral gap of the migration topology's Markov chain, not by where or how often migration occurs. This is structurally parallel to Ghani et al.'s result that the approximation functor $T_\varepsilon$ on open games is lax monoidal --- approximate Nash equilibria fail to compose like exact ones~\cite{ghani2024}. Both instantiate a general pattern: \emph{relaxation of exactness conditions produces uniform laxity in functorial composition}.

\textbf{Result 3: Three-Level Composition.}
The operator $\to$ pipeline $\to$ strategy tower is not merely a design pattern. Each level is a category; inclusions are functors; strategy combinators (hourglass, island, adaptive) are endofunctors on $\mathbf{Strat}$; migration is a natural transformation between island configurations.

\textbf{Positioning.}
Evolutionary computation has never appeared at ACT. We connect it to three active threads: (1)~Ghani's compositional game theory~\cite{ghani2024} --- GAs are open games; the Dichotomy Theorem is the evolutionary analogue of lax composition of approximate equilibria; (2)~Hedges and Sakamoto's categorical cybernetics~\cite{hedges2024} --- GAs are bidirectional processes (forward: genetic operators; backward: fitness-based selection) that may admit an optic description; (3)~Hanks et al.'s optimization-as-functor~\cite{hanks2024} --- we cover the complementary gradient-free regime. The framework is implemented in Haskell (12 modules, 75 tests, ${\sim}5500$ lines) and demonstrated on three domains. A full manuscript is in preparation.
