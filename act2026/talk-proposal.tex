\section{Motivation and Framework}

Every genetic algorithm follows the same compositional pattern: evaluate fitness, select survivors, recombine, mutate, repeat. This structure is invariant across domains---whether the genome encodes game-playing strategy weights~\cite{samuel1959}, maze topologies, or symbolic expressions---yet it remains implicit in imperative implementations. We make it explicit by formalizing GA operators as morphisms in a Kleisli category.

GA operators require randomness, read-only configuration, and write-only logging. These effects are captured by a monad $M = \mathsf{ReaderT}\;\mathit{Config}\;(\mathsf{WriterT}\;\mathit{Log}\;(\mathsf{State}\;\mathit{StdGen}))$, and a \emph{genetic operator} is a Kleisli arrow $\mathit{op} \colon [a] \to M\,[b]$ on populations. A single generation is the composite $\mathit{step} = \mathit{evaluate} \ggg \mathit{select} \ggg \mathit{crossover} \ggg \mathit{mutate}$, itself a Kleisli arrow $[a] \to M\,[a]$, so generations compose: an $n$-generation run is $\mathit{step}^n$. We identify three composition levels:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Operators} compose into \textbf{pipelines} (one generation).
  \item \textbf{Pipelines} compose across generations into \textbf{strategies}.
  \item \textbf{Strategies} compose via higher-order combinators into \textbf{multi-population systems}.
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
Each level has the same categorical structure. Strategy combinators (island, hourglass, adaptive) are endofunctors on $\mathbf{Strat}$; migration is a natural transformation between island configurations.

\section{Results}

We demonstrate three results across three domains (checkers weight evolution, maze topology evolution, symbolic regression) in a Haskell implementation (12 modules, 75 tests, ${\sim}5500$ lines).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Result 1: Diversity Fingerprints.}
Different strategy compositions produce qualitatively distinct diversity trajectories that are \emph{stable across genome types}: monotonic decline (flat/generational), spike--crash--rebound (hourglass), stable maintenance (island), and spike--collapse (adaptive). These signatures are determined by the composition pattern, not by the individual operators or genome type. We conjecture that fingerprints are functorial, composing predictably when strategies are composed---analogous to behavioral invariants of composed dynamical systems~\cite{baez2022}.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Result 2: The Strict/Lax Dichotomy.}
The island model defines a functor $I \colon \mathbf{Strat} \to \mathbf{Strat}$ lifting single-population to multi-population strategies with periodic migration.

\begin{theorem}[Strict/Lax Dichotomy]
For strategies $S_1, S_2$ and island functor $I_{\mu,f}$ with migration rate $\mu$:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item If $\mu = 0$: $I(S_1 \ggg S_2) = I(S_1) \ggg I(S_2)$ \emph{(strict monoidal)}.
  \item If $\mu > 0$: $I(S_1 \ggg S_2) \ncong I(S_1) \ggg I(S_2)$, with laxity magnitude $D^*$ independent of boundary position and migration frequency \emph{(uniformly lax)}.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\noindent
The laxity arises from chaotic amplification of migration schedule perturbation at composition boundaries. This is structurally analogous to Ghani et al.'s result that approximate Nash equilibria fail to compose~\cite{ghani2018}: both instantiate the general phenomenon that lax monoidal functors do not preserve composition strictly.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Result 3: Three-Level Composition.}
The operator $\to$ pipeline $\to$ strategy tower is categorical: each level forms a category, inclusions are functors, and the structure is preserved at every level. This enables recursive reasoning about evolutionary systems.

\section{Positioning at ACT}

This work connects evolutionary computation---a field not previously represented at ACT---to several active threads:

\begin{itemize}[nosep,leftmargin=1.5em]
  \item \textbf{Compositional game theory}~\cite{ghani2018}: GAs are open games; the Dichotomy Theorem is the evolutionary analogue of the failure of approximate equilibria to compose.
  \item \textbf{Categorical cybernetics}~\cite{hedges2023}: GAs are bidirectional (forward: operators; backward: selection). Optics unify our Kleisli and co-Kleisli perspectives.
  \item \textbf{Polynomial dynamical systems}~\cite{spivak2022}: Diversity fingerprints parallel how behavioral invariants of composed systems compose functorially.
\end{itemize}

\noindent
Evolutionary computation offers rich, well-understood examples where categorical formalization yields non-obvious results---the Dichotomy Theorem is not discoverable without the functorial framework.
